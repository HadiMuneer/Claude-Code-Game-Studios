# Game Concept: TIME-PUNK

*Created: 2026-03-28*
*Status: Draft*

---

## Elevator Pitch

> You are a ghost operative who steals a prototype time machine from a criminal vice head of state, then runs 7 timeline missions — using stealth, investigation, and social manipulation in equal measure — to surgically merge fractured timelines and erase a global criminal conspiracy before it ever formed.
>
> But the more you act, the less you can trust your own map: every failure corrupts your information, and every NPC you manipulate may be manipulating you back.

## Core Fantasy

You are the one person who knows the full shape of the conspiracy — past, present, and every fractured timeline in between. You do not have an army. You have information, patience, and the ability to be whoever the moment demands. The fantasy is not power; it is *precision*. The thrill of being the quiet force that erases a criminal empire from history, one surgically placed intervention at a time — and the creeping dread of never being entirely certain your map of reality is still real.

This is a game where knowing *what to trust* is the ultimate skill. No other game gives you a world that lies back at you.

---

## Unique Hook

Like *Hitman 3* AND ALSO *Return of the Obra Dinn* — but your information system actively degrades based on your own failures AND the deliberate deception of your enemies, forcing you to become a forensic analyst of your own paranoia while executing precision operations across fractured time.

The hook: **your map of reality is a resource that both you and your enemies can corrupt.** Learning to read the source of that corruption — self-inflicted erosion vs. NPC counter-intelligence — is the game's deepest skill expression.

## Player Experience Analysis (MDA Framework)

### Target Aesthetics (What the player FEELS)

| Aesthetic | Priority | How We Deliver It |
| ---- | ---- | ---- |
| **Narrative** (drama, story arc) | 1 | Timeline conspiracy arc; every mission advances or complicates the story |
| **Discovery** (exploration, secrets) | 2 | Anomaly detection; map reveals; the gap between predicted and observed reality |
| **Fantasy** (make-believe, role-playing) | 3 | Ghost operative with time machine; false identities; social chameleon |
| **Challenge** (obstacle course, mastery) | 4 | Dual-source corruption detection; mastery arc from chaos to surgical precision |
| **Sensation** (sensory pleasure) | 5 | Noir atmosphere; reactive audio; visual map corruption effects |
| **Expression** (self-expression) | 6 | Three equal verb paths — no canonical solution exists |
| **Fellowship** (social connection) | N/A | Single-player; no multiplayer |
| **Submission** (relaxation) | N/A | Intentionally high-tension; not a comfort game |

### Key Dynamics (Emergent player behaviors)

- Players will develop personal "corruption signatures" — learning to recognize the visual/data patterns of their own failure vs. NPC deception
- Players will experiment with all three verb paths per mission before committing to a strategy
- Players will voluntarily revisit timeline nodes to perform surgical repairs, even when not required, because map clarity feels intrinsically rewarding
- Players will share anomaly discoveries and conspiracy maps with the community (strong social content without multiplayer)
- Players will replay missions to experience "clean" vs. "corrupted" versions of the same events

### Core Mechanics (Systems we build)

1. **Tri-Verb Rotation** — Stealth (movement, cover, timing), Investigation (evidence connection, deduction), and Social Manipulation (dialogue reads, identity construction, counter-deception) operate as equal peers. Every objective can be approached via any verb or a combination.
2. **Fractured Reality Map** — A visual timeline map with three states: pre-mission fog (incomplete data, player guesses entry point), in-mission ripple (real-time consequence propagation from every decision), post-mission analysis (compare predicted map vs. observed reality, surface anomalies).
3. **Dual-Source Corruption System** — Map erosion has two distinct origins: self-inflicted (stealth failures, investigation errors, social missteps create noise points; facts become probabilities, nodes ghost) and external (NPCs engaged in counter-deception feed false data into the map, planting ghosts of what they want you to believe). Distinguishing the source is the game's advanced skill layer.
4. **Timeline Repair Economy** — Players can spend time resources to perform surgical repairs at earlier timeline nodes, trading forward momentum for map clarity. This creates a constant risk/reward calculus: push forward degraded or spend resources to restore information trust.
5. **NPC Psychology States** — Each NPC has readable behavioral tells, psychological vulnerabilities, and (for adversarial NPCs) counter-deception capabilities. Social manipulation is bidirectional — the player reads NPCs and NPCs read the player.

## Player Motivation Profile

### Primary Psychological Needs Served

| Need | How This Game Satisfies It | Strength |
| ---- | ---- | ---- |
| **Autonomy** (freedom, meaningful choice) | Three equal verb paths; no canonical solution; timeline repair is always optional | Core |
| **Competence** (mastery, skill growth) | Mastery arc from paranoid improvisation to surgical precision; corruption source detection as deep skill | Core |
| **Relatedness** (connection, belonging) | Narrative weight of every NPC interaction; the conspiracy's human cost made tangible through investigation | Supporting |

### Player Type Appeal (Bartle Taxonomy)

- [x] **Achievers** — Perfecting a clean timeline (zero corruption) as a mastery goal; mission ratings
- [x] **Explorers** — Anomaly discovery; the gap between predicted and observed reality; hidden conspiracy threads
- [ ] **Socializers** — Not served directly; single-player
- [ ] **Killers/Competitors** — Not served; no PvP

**Primary**: Explorers + Storytellers (Quantic Foundry: Story + Mastery + Immersion)
**Secondary**: Achievers pursuing corruption-clean runs

### Flow State Design

- **Onboarding curve**: Prologue mission (island infiltration) introduces all three verbs in a controlled sequence before the timeline mechanic is introduced. Players learn each verb in isolation, then in combination, before corruption is added.
- **Difficulty scaling**: Early missions use mostly self-inflicted corruption (controllable); later missions introduce NPC counter-deception (requires forensic reading skill). The mastery arc from "what's happening?" to "I can read the source" mirrors the player's real skill growth.
- **Feedback clarity**: The visual map is the primary feedback system — map clarity directly reflects player performance. Corruption is visible, readable, and satisfying to repair. Anomalies surface as narrative discoveries, making "failure" feel like story content.
- **Recovery from failure**: The Fractured Reality system — failure is never a hard stop. Noise points accumulate, map degrades, but the player always continues. Recovery is an active choice (spend time resources on surgical repair) not a forced reset.

## Core Loop

### Moment-to-Moment (30 seconds)

Rapid tri-verb rotation. The player is simultaneously: reading an NPC's behavioral tells during a dialogue beat, repositioning through cover as a patrol pattern shifts, and mentally updating their understanding of a conspiracy node based on a just-discovered document. The game never allows the player to settle into a single mode — each verb creates pressure on the other two, and the rotation itself is the primary cognitive satisfaction.

### Short-Term (5–15 minutes)

Decision node → immediate timeline ripple → forced adaptation. The player makes a meaningful choice (eliminate a guard, steal a file, plant a false identity with an NPC), sees the immediate cascade on their live map, and must adapt their strategy to the new configuration. Each node resolution unlocks the next cluster of choices. The "one more node" loop is the primary engagement driver.

### Session-Level (60–120 minutes)

**Pre-mission**: Player reviews the fogged timeline map, interprets coded data hints, and commits to a time entry point — a deliberate gamble under incomplete information (noir atmosphere of the unknown).

**In-mission**: Tri-verb rotation with live consequence management. The map updates in real-time as the player acts. Mistakes accumulate as noise points; NPC counter-deception may silently plant false data. The player finishes the mission with a map that reflects everything they did.

**Post-mission**: Full analysis interface opens. Player compares predicted map vs. observed reality, surfaces anomalies ("the map says this person is dead, but they were standing in front of me"), and advances the conspiracy narrative. This is where the story breathes and the next mission hook forms.

Natural stopping point: post-mission analysis complete, next mission's fog map revealed, entry point decision looming.

### Long-Term Progression

The player's arc mirrors the game's emotional design: early missions are paranoid improvisation under high uncertainty; late missions are precise surgical intervention — earned through deep system understanding. The timeline itself is the long-term goal: 7 missions to merge the fractured timelines and erase the conspiracy. But the meta-goal is mastery: the ability to read a corrupted map and know exactly why it looks the way it does.

**Progression axes**: Map literacy (reading corruption sources), social profiling (NPC psychology depth), timeline knowledge (understanding which nodes are structurally load-bearing for the conspiracy).

### Retention Hooks

- **Curiosity**: Post-mission anomalies plant questions that can only be answered in the next mission. The gap between predicted and observed reality is a constant narrative engine.
- **Investment**: A corrupted map is a record of everything the player has done. Repairing it feels personal. The conspiracy's human cost compounds across missions.
- **Social**: Anomaly screenshots and timeline map states are highly shareable community content — without requiring multiplayer.
- **Mastery**: The corruption-source detection skill has a long ceiling. Players return to replay missions "cleanly" once they understand the system.

## Game Pillars

### Pillar 1: Every Action Echoes

No choice is isolated. Every stealth decision, investigation discovery, and social manipulation move marks the timeline map. The world does not forget.

*Design test*: If we're debating whether a minor NPC interaction should affect the map, this pillar says it must. Nothing is cosmetic.

### Pillar 2: Information is Power, Corrupted

The player's map is their most valuable and most fragile resource. Trust nothing completely — including the data your own failures have produced.

*Design test*: If we're adding a UI element that makes the player's information state cleaner or more certain, this pillar asks whether that clarity has been earned. Unearned clarity violates the pillar.

### Pillar 3: Three Paths, One Truth

Stealth, investigation, and social manipulation are equally valid routes to every objective. No solution is canonical. No verb is subordinate.

*Design test*: If a mission objective can only be completed via stealth (or only via social manipulation), this pillar says it's broken. Every objective must have at least two verb paths.

### Pillar 4: Earn the Knife

Surgical precision is the reward for mastery, never the default. Players begin in paranoid improvisation and work toward controlled intervention. The game never hands them a clean map.

*Design test*: If an early-game system makes the player feel competent and in control without earning it, this pillar says dial it back. Comfort is a late-game reward.

### Pillar 5: The Weight Stays

Tone, pacing, audio, and feedback reinforce consequence at every moment. Nothing feels trivial. Small victories carry the cost of getting there. The game never lets the player feel light.

*Design test*: If a piece of UI feedback, audio cue, or dialogue line feels flip or consequence-free, this pillar says it needs redesign. The noir melancholy is not aesthetic — it is systemic.

### Pillar 6: Truth is a Fractured Mirror

Social interaction is not a dialogue tree — it is a data source. NPCs are adversarial information nodes. Lie too much and the world lies back, feeding the visual map ghosts of what it wants you to believe. Map corruption has two distinct origins: self-inflicted erosion (your own failures) and external erosion (deliberate NPC counter-deception). Learning to distinguish the two is the game's deepest skill.

*Design test*: If an NPC interaction produces information with no possibility of that information being false or source-ambiguous, this pillar says the NPC lacks design depth. Every NPC capable of speech should have a deception potential rating.

### Anti-Pillars (What This Game Is NOT)

- **NOT a power fantasy**: The player is capable but fragile — never invincible. Adding abilities or systems that make the player feel dominant over the world violates the emotional register.
- **NOT a puzzle with one correct solution**: Every objective has multiple verb paths. Designing a "correct" solution removes player agency and invalidates Pillar 3.
- **NOT a "retry until clean" game**: Failure leaves permanent marks. Systems that erase player mistakes (undo mechanics, consequence-free reloads) undermine the Fractured Reality system's emotional weight.
- **NOT an action game with stealth as option**: Stealth is a discipline, not a skip button. Adding combat systems that make violence a dominant strategy would collapse the three-verb balance.

## Risks and Open Questions

### Design Risks

- **Tri-verb balance**: If one verb is significantly more efficient than the others in practice, players will default to it and the three-path promise collapses. Requires careful mission design and playtesting.
- **Corruption readability**: Dual-source corruption is the game's deepest skill — but if players can't learn to distinguish the sources, the system produces frustration rather than mastery. The feedback design for this is critical and unproven.
- **Information density**: The game runs on complex information systems. Onboarding must deliver enough scaffolding that players feel oriented without removing the intended uncertainty.
- **Tone maintenance**: Eight missions of sustained noir melancholy is a design discipline. Tone drift (moments of levity that break the register) will erode the emotional architecture.

### Technical Risks

- **3D third-person stealth AI**: NavMesh pathfinding + perception cones + behavioral state machines in Godot 4.6 is the highest-complexity system. No existing template matches this exact configuration. Requires early prototyping.
- **Timeline map UI**: The fog/ripple/corruption visualization is a custom Godot UI system with no direct template. Must be prototyped early — it is the game's primary feedback surface.
- **Godot 4.4–4.6 knowledge gap**: The LLM's training data covers Godot to ~4.3. Jolt physics (4.4+, default in 4.6), D3D12 default on Windows (4.6), and shader changes require populated engine reference docs before implementation begins.
- **NPC psychology state machine**: Full bidirectional deception capability (NPCs read and deceive the player) is a non-trivial AI system. Scope risk if this exceeds complexity estimates.

### Market Risks

- **Niche audience**: The target player (Disco Elysium / Obra Dinn audience) is small but deeply loyal and high-conversion. Commercial success requires community evangelism, not broad reach.
- **Time-travel narrative complexity**: Games with complex time mechanics have mixed commercial track records. The map system must make the complexity legible, or the narrative loses the audience.

### Scope Risks

- **8 missions for a "few months" timeline**: This is ambitious. The Shippable tier (5–6 missions) is the realistic ship target. Full Vision (8 missions) requires timeline discipline.
- **Social manipulation as full peer system**: Content-heavy. Each NPC needs psychology profile, deception potential rating, and behavioral tell design. This is the most labor-intensive content system.
- **Art pipeline**: 3D stylized art for 8 missions with ~15–20 NPCs is a significant art investment for a small team.

### Open Questions

- **How does the player learn to distinguish corruption sources?** — What visual or data signatures differentiate self-inflicted erosion from NPC counter-deception? Requires dedicated UX prototyping with test players.
- **What is the exact time repair economy?** — How many "repair actions" per mission? What is the cost unit? How does it feel to spend a repair? Requires gameplay prototyping to find the right tension point.
- **How much social manipulation content per mission?** — Each mission needs enough NPC psychology depth for social manipulation to feel like a real path. Minimum NPC count per mission needs a design spec.
- **Does the prologue mission need to teach the map system, or is discovery of the map system part of the first timeline mission's design?** — Affects onboarding architecture significantly.

## MVP Definition

**Core hypothesis**: Players find the tri-verb rotation combined with the Fractured Reality map system engaging for 60+ minute sessions, and the corruption mechanic produces paranoid investment rather than frustration.

**Required for MVP**:
1. Prologue mission fully playable — all three verbs functional, clean map (no corruption introduced), teaches each verb in sequence and combination
2. One timeline mission with live map ripple, self-inflicted corruption, and post-mission analysis interface
3. Basic Fractured Reality visualization — noise points, ghosted nodes, fact-to-probability degradation
4. At least one NPC with full psychology state and social manipulation path
5. Post-mission deduction interface — compare predicted vs. observed, surface one anomaly

**Explicitly NOT in MVP** (defer to later):
- NPC counter-deception / external corruption source (implement after self-inflicted corruption is validated)
- Timeline repair economy (implement after corruption system is stable)
- Full audio adaptive system
- Missions 2–7
- Multiple NPC types per mission

### Scope Tiers

| Tier | Content | Features | Target Timeline |
| ---- | ---- | ---- | ---- |
| **MVP** | Prologue + 1 timeline mission | Tri-verb, basic Fractured Reality (self-inflicted only), post-mission analysis | 4–6 weeks |
| **Vertical Slice** | Prologue + 2–3 missions | Full dual-source corruption, NPC counter-deception, timeline repair economy | 8–12 weeks |
| **Shippable** | Prologue + 5–6 missions | All systems, adaptive audio, full NPC deception ecosystem | 16–20 weeks |
| **Full Vision** | Prologue + 7 missions | All content, polished corruption visualization, complete conspiracy arc | 24–28 weeks |
